BMW M8 Gran Coupe - The Ultimate Driving Machine

Home · Specs · Reviews

BMW introduced the M8 Gran Coupé Concept, which previewed a high performance four-door variant of the Concept 8 Series at the the 2017 Frankfurt Motor Show. It generated a lot of interest among car enthusists. Finally roduction began in August 2019.The BMW M8 Gran Coupe is one of the most prestigious and luxurious sports car that BMW currently offers. And it is not just a delight to look at. It is also a delight to drive around. The car is guaranteed to get you attention while driving it around both in the city and in the highways. Well this should come as no surprise given how stunningly gorgeous it looks.Under the hood is a silky 617-hp twin-turbo V-8 engine, a snappy eight-speed automatic, and an all-wheel-drive system with a rear-drive mode that enables drifts and burnouts.When it comes to competition it stands against other big-mile GT bruisers like Mercedes’ AMG GT 4-door Coupe, the Porsche Panamera and Bentley’s Flying Spur.The M8 has the ability to satisfy a lot of our expectations. And it rightly should. It doesn't come cheap. With a hefty starting price of around $130,000 the M8 goes on to satisfy a lot of our expectations if not all. Let us look at those in detail.

Exterior

The M8 is a 4 door - sedan and pretty big one at it. The fit and finish are what you would expect from such a luxurious car. They are perfect. The iconic front grill, the BMW logo and the projection headlamps upfront make it look really stunning. And of course like most other sports cars you can always go for after market modifications to make it look even more menacing. The model of course comes in plenty of color options. Red, Blue and Green colors make the car stand out even more. The car looks absolutely stunning no matter which angle you look at it from. The beefy rear portion with gorgeous back lights and the stunning alloy wheels are the cherry on top.

Interior

The M8 Gran Coupe features an exquisite interior that looks as rich as it feels. While the design is more dignified than dazzling, the four-door M8 provides plentiful rear passenger space. It also offers tons of desirable standard features such as a 12.3-inch digital gauge cluster, 16-way power front seats with memory settings, heating, and ventilation, four-zone climate control, leather upholstery, and more.Buyers can also enhance the cabin with novel color schemes and fancy trims as well as a Comfort Seating package. This adds heated seats throughout along with power sunshades for rear-seat passengers. While the back seat features three seatbelts.

The Gran Coupe is quite a bit longer than the 8 Series Coupe and the widest car that BMW currently makes, so space inside for four adults is good. Those in that front can stretch out, while only the very tall will complain about headroom in the rear. Every M8 Gran Coupe is equipped with a 10.3-inch touchscreen infotainment system that can also be operated with a rotary controller on the center console. Along with its 16-speaker Harman/Kardon sound system, the brawny BMW has a Wi-Fi hotspot and Apple CarPlay and Android Auto as standard.

Performance

The M8 Gran Coupe is powered by a lusty twin-turbo 4.4-liter V-8 that makes 617 horsepower.The exceptionally smooth engine sends that power to all four wheels via a responsive eight-speed automatic transmission. However, tail-out antics are made possible by a selectable rear-wheel-drive mode that routes all the V-8's fury to the back. This rocket-powered four-door shot to 60 mph in just 2.7 seconds and completed the quarter-mile in 10.8 ticks at 129 mph.While it's furiously quick and provides more than 1.0 g of cornering grip, the big M8 suffers from indifferent steering feel and a stiff ride that compromises its comfort. A specially tuned adaptive suspension comes standard, and it can be adjusted through the sedan's customizable or preset drive modes, but even the softest damper setting isn't soft at all.

With estimates of 15 mpg in the city and 21 mpg on the highway, the V-8-powered M8 Gran Coupe certainly won't be confused for a hybrid. However, those figures are on par with other fast four-doors in this class. Now coming to the way it sounds. And boy does it sound good. The V-8 engine roars like a proper powerful sports car. Don't belive me? Well here it yourself.

Final thoughts

BMW have a winner with respect to the M8 Gran Coupe. Although on the pricey side this is definitely a car to consider if one can afford it. It offers a unique experience of comining fun with elegance and luxury.Whether you will be riding the car or travelling as a pillion. And you surely won't be disappointed.
